A modular assistant that turns plain ideas into structured research designs

Functions

  • Transforms unstructured ideas into structured research designs
  • Automatically generates design specs by project type (academic, survey, evaluation, desktop research)
  • Drafts conceptual models, hypotheses, survey outlines, and logic models
  • Creates visual conceptual diagrams (Mermaid/GoJS)
  • Suggests next-step GPT workflows for analysis or writing

Target users

  • Academic researchers and graduate students
  • Survey and evaluation specialists
  • Research consultants
  • R&D and policy planners

Ideal use cases

  • Developing a full research design from an initial idea or hypothesis
  • Automating program evaluation or survey study design
  • Structuring a desktop research plan with objectives, scope, and source strategy

Limitations

  • Does not perform statistical analyses or interpret empirical results
  • Cannot revise models based on data (requires follow-up GPT such as ResRefiner)
  • Does not conduct literature or source synthesis (use LitSearch or SourceFinder GPTs)

Example commands

  • Turn ‘the effect of digital literacy on university students’ academic achievement’ into a research design.
  • Create a YAML-format evaluation design for a youth policy program.
  • Design a desktop research project on climate technology market trends.

Additional features

  • Multilingual support (English, Korean, etc.)
  • Structured YAML pipeline output
  • Automatic project-type detection and tailored design generation
  • Modular design engine (academic_research, survey_project, program_evaluation, desktop_research)
