A research-focused GPT that safely searches, filters, and structures scholarly and policy literature based on trusted sources.

Functions

  • Searches scholarly and policy literature based on user-provided topics and keywords with a strong focus on source reliability.
  • Organizes and summarizes retrieved documents by theme, source type, and reliability so they are easy to reuse in research and policy analysis.

Target users

  • Researchers and graduate students preparing academic papers or reports
  • Policy analysts and public-sector researchers conducting evidence-based policy studies

Ideal use cases

  • When you need a structured overview of key academic and institutional literature on a specific research topic
  • When drafting policy or research reports that require trustworthy academic articles and international/national institutional reports as evidence

Limitations

  • It cannot directly access paywalled databases or individual full-text PDFs and relies only on information and summaries that are publicly accessible.

Example commands

  • Find academic papers and international organization reports on "Korea's carbon neutrality policy" in safe mode and summarize them by research topic, method, and key findings.
  • For the topic "digital transformation and labor market inequality", collect major academic and institutional literature from the last 5–10 years and provide an overview grouped by discipline (e.g., economics, sociology).

Additional features

  • Provides a smart literature search protocol with modes such as safe, flexible, exploratory, and institutional_scan that adjust source types and reliability criteria.
