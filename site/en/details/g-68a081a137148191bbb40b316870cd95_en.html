GroupCompare AI · (자동번역) GroupCompare AI

Automatically designs and runs group-difference, equivalence (TOST), and non-inferiority tests on uploaded data, and reports multiplicity-adjusted results with effect sizes, CIs, and power/MDE in APA style.

Functions

  • Data preprocessing & diagnostics (missingness/outliers/skewness/cluster-panel detection) with actionable recommendations
  • Automatic test selection & execution for group comparisons (t/Welch/Mann–Whitney, ANOVA/Welch ANOVA, chi-square/exact tests, etc.)
  • Covariate adjustment (ANCOVA) and clustered/panel data handling recommendations (cluster-robust SE, mixed-effects models)
  • Multiple-testing correction (Holm default; Bonferroni/BH options) with controlled correction scope
  • Effect sizes with confidence intervals (Cohen d/Hedges g, η²/ω², risk difference/RR/OR, etc.) and interpretation templates
  • Equivalence & non-inferiority testing (TOST with 90% CI) using configurable equivalence margins (Δ)
  • Power & sample planning (a priori/post hoc power, MDE) with automatic low-power warnings
  • Subgroup analysis & interaction checks (interaction p-values, concise heterogeneity summary; forest-plot recommendations)
  • Report generation with summary cards, detailed tables/figures, and appendix (code/logs) in APA statistical reporting style
  • Multilingual reporting support (Korean/English and major SEA languages) via i18n resources

Target users

  • Researchers and data analysts running group comparisons on experiments/surveys/observational data
  • Product and growth teams (A/B testing, multiple metrics, standardized reporting)
  • Academic/policy analysts who need effect sizes, power/MDE, and equivalence claims
  • Consultants/analytics owners who want consistent statistical reporting pipelines

Ideal use cases

  • End-to-end A/B testing reports (significance + effect size + multiplicity correction) for multiple KPIs
  • Make an equivalence claim (not just ‘non-significant’) via TOST with a justified margin Δ
  • Compute MDE and recommend re-testing when power is insufficient
  • Compare effects across countries/segments and assess interactions
  • Estimate adjusted group differences using ANCOVA (e.g., baseline-controlled outcomes)

Limitations

  • Equivalence/non-inferiority conclusions are highly sensitive to the chosen margin Δ; unjustified margins can undermine interpretability.
  • Causal interpretation is limited for observational data due to potential confounding; additional design/estimation strategies may be required.
  • Small samples, rare events, or complex clustering may require exact tests or advanced mixed-model specifications beyond simple defaults.

Example commands

  • Compare CTR and CVR between variants A and B in my uploaded data, apply Holm correction, and report effect sizes, 95% CIs, and MDE.
  • Test the mean difference in influencer_credibility between male and female; switch to Welch if variances differ.
  • Run ANOVA across 5 countries with post-hoc tests; propose robust alternatives if normality is severely violated.
  • Check whether A and B are equivalent using TOST (Δ: Cohen d=0.2) and report the decision clearly.
  • Run subgroup analysis by age group × country and report interaction p-values and a heterogeneity summary.

Additional features

  • Default settings: alpha=0.05, CI=95%, TOST CI=90%, power target=0.80, Holm correction by default
  • UI toggles: plain-language vs technical summary, equivalence margin (Δ) on/off, correction method, subgroup analysis, language switch
  • Outputs: summary card (decision/effect size/MDE/recommendations) + detailed tables/figures + appendix (code/logs)
