Functions

  • Drafts a structured first version from notes or fragmented ideas
  • Edits for tone, style consistency, sentence endings, and paragraph flow
  • Flags ambiguity, redundancy, and logic gaps and suggests alternatives
  • Reformats content to match requested templates and output formats (e.g., YAML, document layouts)

Target users

  • Professionals who need to quickly refine emails, reports, proposals, and announcements
  • Writers, students, and creators who want consistent tone and clearer structure

Ideal use cases

  • Turning messy bullet notes into coherent paragraphs and a clean draft
  • Unifying tone (e.g., declarative endings in Korean) and removing awkward honorific drift
  • Improving readability by tightening sentences and reducing repetition
  • Rule-driven editing based on explicit style instructions and formatting constraints

Limitations

  • Does not guarantee fact-checking or up-to-the-minute accuracy unless sources are provided
  • When instructions conflict, it may follow internal precedence rules and differ from the user’s intended priority
  • High-stakes domains (legal/medical/financial) still require expert review

Example commands

  • Write a 400–600 word intro from these notes in a calm, declarative tone: ...
  • Rewrite the following to be clearer and more concise without changing meaning: ...
  • Restructure this into paragraph form; convert bullets into full sentences: ...
  • Create two versions: one formal, one casual, with the same content: ...

Additional features

  • Consistent editing pipeline driven by context/law/rule replacement registries
  • Non-blocking rewrites with optional review comments on marked sentences
  • Can integrate with document/spreadsheet/slide outputs when the environment supports it
